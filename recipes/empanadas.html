Meat (Pine) Empanadas (Chilean recipe)

Description

Chilean empanadas are undoubtedly a fundamental dish in any kitchen in our country . A few days after beginning to celebrate a new month of the country , these delicacies are beginning to take on importance in the daily diet of Chileans.

But given this growing popularity of its flavor and seasonings, what is the origin of our empanada? For that, we must cross the Atlantic and reach Spain, the motherland. There were the Arabs who, after leaving Hispanic territory, left this type of food as a culinary heritage. At the end of the process of expulsion of the Moors, the discovery of America occurred and it is on this continent where the explosion occurred in terms of variety and types of ingredients. This is how the Chilean, Tucumán, Salteña, allaca, arepa or pacucapa empanadas were born, among many more.

However, it was here in Chile where meat, onion and seasonings became an irresistible delicacy for the first settlers and residents of our country.

The origin of the pine

But the ingredient that gives all the flavor and color to our empanada is the tasty pine. The perfect mix between onion, beef, olive, egg and raisins was a mixture of various ingredients found in our country that were added by the Spanish to make the empanada. Previous antecedents indicate that the Mapuche called this mixture "Pirru" which later resulted in what we know today as pine.

The "new" empanadas

Despite all the progress that traditional pine varieties have gained, new flavors and tastes have emerged in the Chilean market. The Neapolitan - which is a kind of wrapped pizza -, shrimp cheese, mushroom cheese, macha cheese, an assortment of seafood, sweets, with cream cheese and an endless list. But despite its endless options, the empanda is, was and will be, the great queen of every National Holiday celebration.

Ingredients

    For the mass:

  • 600g flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 120g vegetable butter (melted and warm)
  • 40ml white wine
  • Warm water

    For the filling:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 onions chopped into fine cubes
  • 500g meat in very small cubes (pink or black meat)
  • 1/2 teaspoon Garlic Powder
  • 1 teaspoon Color Chili Pepper
  • 1 teaspoon Whole Oregano
  • 1 teaspoon Ground Cumin
  • Salt
  • Ground Black Pepper
  • 24 blonde raisins (optional)
  • 12 black olives
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs cut into quarters
  • 2 eggs lightly beaten to paint

Preparation

How to make the dough for the traditional Chilean empanada?

Mix the flour and salt with the butter in a bowl and integrate with your hands to form crumbs. Add the wine and enough warm water (approximately 3/4 cup) until you have a soft but not sticky dough. Transfer the dough to a floured counter and knead for about 5 minutes or until you have a smooth and elastic dough. Let it rest covered while you prepare the filling.

How to make the pine oven empanada?

Prepare the filling: Heat the oil in a frying pan, add the onion and cook until it is transparent and well cooked (it should not brown). Add the meat, Garlic Powder, Color Chili Pepper, Whole Oregano and Ground Cumin ; cook until the meat is cooked. Finally add the salt and Ground Black Pepper to taste. Let cool.

Assembling empanada

To assemble the empanadas: Spread the dough on a floured counter to approximately 1/2 cm thick. Cut circles about 15 cm in diameter. Then fill each empanada with a tablespoon of pine, 2 raisins, an olive and a piece of hard-boiled egg. Moisten the edges of the dough with a little water, close and fold the edges, flatten slightly to seal the edges. Brush the empanadas with the beaten egg, place them on a baking tray lightly greased with oil or butter. Prick them twice with a cocktail stick and place them in the oven preheated to 180ºC for about 40 minutes or until the dough is cooked and golden.
